3 Hidden Historical Facts You Didnt Know About Italys History.

Pasta wasnt always made with tomatoes, in fact tomatoes arrived very late.

Tomatoes are native to the Americas and only reached Italy in the 16th century. For centuries before that, Italians ate pasta with cheese, pepper, or other sauces—no marinara in sight.

Rome had a fire department… run by a private millionaire?

In ancient Rome, Marcus Licinius Crassus—one of the richest men in history— created his own fire brigade. When a building caught fire, his team would show up and only put it out if the owner agreed to sell the property to Crassus at a discounted price.

Venice once declared war on an entire sea.

Venice once symbolically declared war on the entire Adriatic Sea. In the early form of a ritual that later became the Marriage of the Sea ceremony, the Venetian Republic performed a public act meant to assert its dominance over the water after storms had damaged its ships. This evolved into the tradition where the doge threw a ring into the sea to show that Venice claimed authority over it.
